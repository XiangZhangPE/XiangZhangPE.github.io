Introduction

I have accomplished my Power Electronics M.A.Sc. degree from Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering in Queen's University, working under the mentorship of Dr. Praveen K. Jain and Dr. Shangzhi Pan. Currently, I am an incomming PHD student in CPES of Virginia Tech with Dr. Qiang Li.

My research involves PV micro inverters, coupled magnetics application in resonant converters, current sharing techniques of multiphase converter, and high precise digital control.

Prior in my research journey, I obtained my bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Wuhan University advised by Dr. Meng Huang.

Outside of academia, I like photographing, aerial filming, and Soulslike games.

Research Interests

Power Electronics: From grid-scale to power modules;
Digital Control Techniques: Adaptive voltage positioning with high dynamic responds, trajectory control for multiphase converter, time domain analysis based resonant converters, and asymmetry PWM modulations;
Complex Magnetics Coupling in Power Converters: Symmetrical coupled inductors' behavior and their potential in current/voltage balancing, multiport active bridge (MAB) transformer, power decoupling, and high density magnetic integration;
Resonant Converters: High frequency resonant converter and its application in PV micro inverters, Multilevel-modular Converters, and computation systems' VR modules;
